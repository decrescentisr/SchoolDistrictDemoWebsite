Social Media

Social media is crucial to our technology standards and educators are encouraged to include social media as part of their daily curriculum.











School District covers over 50,000 residents and 10,000 students. We have 2 high schools, 4 middle schools and 12 elementary schools.


Social Media


Twitter


Students have the option to register for Twitter accounts (with parental permission) which will allow them to connect socially with other students around the world. Age controls and restrictions will apply.

Facebook


Facebook is one of the most modern-era frequently visited social media platforms. Students have the option to register for a profile and interact with their class and other students. Age controls and restrictions will apply.

Instagram


Instagram is the increasingly popular social media image site that allows users to upload selfies, and personal images to share with users. Students will have the option to join instagram and interact with other users. Age controls and restrictions will apply.

YouTube


Students will have the option to share appropriate videos and incorporate them with projects and other media presentations. Age controls and restrictions will apply.



Why Social Media?

Social media provides students the ability to interact with other children and students across the world. It provides a social aspect that was not invented when their parents were children. With the guidance of parents and educators, social media is a great tool for social learning!.

Age Appropriate

In today's era, the dangers of social media predators are on the rise. That is why we take it seriously and implement age-appropriate tools so that our students are not in danger. Our student's safety is our #1 concern!

Platforms

As stated, students have the option to join Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and or YouTube as part of the social media curriculum requirement. Parent's will receive a parental permission document which states information they have to sign off on to allow their student to participate. More information is in the privacy policy section of the website.

Social media is fun and exciting and meets state standards for technology and social interaction. For more information about state educational standards, please see the state standards located on the state board of education website.
